Geronigo | Case Study

The worlds first international adventure network

For transport theres Uber, for food theres Deliveroo, for beds theres Airbnb, for company in that bed theres tinder! But what about for the things to do? the activities, the adventures and excitment? Introducing Geronigo, 'One World... Every Adventure'.

Imagine you want to jump out of a plane at 14,000 feet, maybe you want to learn to scuba dive, to shoot your mates to bits on a paintball site, play clay pigeon shooting or go karting, bungee jumping, take all those awesome adventures and any others you can possibly think of and you will find them at Geronigo, on an international scale.

Notes from the beginning...

The concept was to take what had already been built with existing activity networks, but give it a bigger more authoritative feel to stand on the international stage.

Standardised design, but with an adventurous edge

The brand design needed to emote adventure, excitement and a world on the edge of every day life, while keeping the UI accessible. How did I achieve this?

  • check
    Logo on a slant with a grunge effect font
  • check
    Torn graphical surrounds of main imagery
  • check
    Emphasis on keywords adventure & explore

Showcase breadth of 100 activities at 5000 locations

The key feature of the site is that you can't find this level of adventure anywhere else. How did I demonstrate that?

  • check
    A montage of activities pulling from the wide range of categories; air, water, combat, extreme to name a few.
  • check
    Animation of montage, to demonstrate joining together of widely different activities.
  • check
    Emphasis and repetition of the phrase 'Every Adventure'.

Limiting choice, leading to bookings

With so much choice, methods needed to be put in place to demonstrate realistic options for the user. How was this achieved?

  • check
    Using GEO location and pictorially list the closest venues nearby
  • check
    Creating a compact tabular list of activities, with venue columns ordered by nearest first.
  • check
    Putting availability details at the core of venue pages.

An appraisal of the current Geronigo

With the first iteration of Geronigo a lot has proven to work, but with any project there is room for improvement.

Concept Successes

  • check
    Logo font and angle represents the 'distressed' concept of the brand well
  • check
    Main montage acts as a good introduction with the right level of distressed design elements to convey adventure.
  • check
    Link structure and SEO are well defined and geared towards future growth.
  • check
    Extensive speed testing will aid in the SEO growth of the website.
  • check
    The nearby venues page does a through job of promoting the multitude of activities and venues available, particular in terms of ranking nearest first.
  • check
    The venue pages convey all require information in a well ordered pattern with booking the central feature.

Scope for improvement

  • check
    The logo font with acitivity silhouettes isnt scalable for different resolutions and sizes.
  • check
    The torn headers on main pages: vouchers, activity, venue - while work in relation to the distressed brand concept of Geronigo, they ultimately cover too much valuable screen real estate.
  • check
    The core css framework needs to transition to bootstrap or similiar to ease maintenance issues and create a more robust core design layout.
  • check
    Core UX principles, material as an example need applying to the website. The main navigation needs moving to the header, with the bottom navbar only used for mobile use.
  • check
    The ability to navigate quickly through activities to find a suitable activity and venue needs improvement.
  • check
    The underlying javascript needs improvement for speed benefits
