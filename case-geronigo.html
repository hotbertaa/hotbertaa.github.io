The worlds first international adventure network

For transport theres Uber, for food theres Deliveroo, for beds theres Airbnb, for company in that bed theres tinder! But what about for the things to do? the activities, the adventures and excitment? Introducing Geronigo, 'One World... Every Adventure'.

The formation of Geronigo

Geronigo's first iteration used the existing design and layout of the companies already established multi activity website theactivitypeople.co.uk. Overiding the Activity Peoples blue and navy theme with its a newer red and black theme.

Emphasis was placed on changing the existing use of language from activities & search to more emotive terms such as adventure and exploring.

User experience issues with Geronigo's 1st iteration

Three key problems presented themselves with the first iteration of Geronigo

The website doesnt convey the companies 100+ range of activities

Half of the header real estate is used as the primary space for activity imagery, with a maximum of 3 activities combined as a montage. The montage images are randomised on page load, but it doesnt suffice to convey the wide range of activities offered.

The venue search doesnt demonstrate the breadth of activities & venues available

The user has to navigate through a conventional and time consuming expandable menu of alternative categories and sub activities, loading one activity at a time.

A better user navigation is needed to filter users to their desired categories & activities

The user navigation focusses the user to use a search function to find venues within their preferred location. This leads to a bottle neck where by the breadth of activities of display doesnt represent the networks vast array.

"Browsing different activities and comparing package cost took a lot of time and I felt I didnt get a full idea of the full range of activities."

Paraphrasing historical user experience feedback

The evolution of Geronigo

Due to already identified user experience issues and the creation of an adventure app a renewal design and new user interface was required

A new brand identity demonstrating the range of activities on offer

A cutout montage of activities was created incorporating activities from each of of our categories; from motor sports, water sports, extreme sports and more. Composition and color balance was a consideration.

At a later date the montage was animated for web and mobile app use.

A renewed navigation to promote categories & activities

How to display 100 activities and 5000 locations on one page?

It's not an easy task. But I think this version was a success and search analytics have shown a greater spread of users exploring different activities. The breadth of activities demonstrated pictorially are clearly displayed in the left column, closely stacked on top of one another. with the volume of related locations neatly nested in the aligned right column in a horizontal scrollable overflow list.

User experience issues with Geronigo's 2nd iteration

Dark needed to become light

Many of the companies activity websites consist of dark themed websites, Geronigo itself was a dark themed site. All industry guidelines state that a light themed website is more trustworthy and accessible to users. Dark elements would be kept as contrast to the main lighter regions.

Venue pages lacked clear focus on how to book an experience at the venue.

Within the hierachy of the venue page, package information and booking availability came after venue details, facilities and imagery. Common user surveying stated that users were unsure how to book online with the result being people calling to book.

A more exciting and interactive voucher purchasing process was needed

The voucher booking process was long, consisting of user details, recipient message information, packaging options and delivery details. Purchasing more than one voucher would increase this further. A similiar more manageable method was needed.

"I wasnt sure where to book my experience online so preferred to call to book my event."

"The voucher booking process felt long especially when purchasing more than one voucher"

Conducted via post booking surveys

Event organisers were invited to complete a survey of their online expeirence using the Geronigo website. Those who completed surveying received a complimentary Geronigo gift voucher.

The current Geronigo

The 3rd iteration of the Geronigo website has presently been live for more than 1 year

Creating an industry standard lighter themed template.

A company preference has been for activity websites to be darker themed. A conserted effort within the design and product development team was pursued to encourage the transition of Geronigo from a dark to a more industry standard lighter theme. Care was taken to keep dark elements to add visual contrast to the website design.

Since changing the site theme to a lighter version google analytics has shown a 20% decrease in bounce rate and an overall improvement in page sessions and duration per user.

Breaking free from a traditional full calendar availability booker

A new method was need to be designed to convey availability per date & session, without the need for a large and space filling calendar. A horizontal scrollable date picker with listed sessions below was design to modernise this issue.

Vertical real-estate was reduced by 50% and the features width allows for better rendering on lower width screen sizes in comparision to 7 day column calendars.

A new voucher booker was created

Voucher basket information was added to the site header which launches a basket modal on selection. Vouchers were thus purchasable from all pages and easy to access. The voucher process was broken down into 4 smaller categories, including a new feature of determining your voucher design of choice.

An appraisal of the current Geronigo

With the 3rd iteration of Geronigo a lot has proven to work, but with any project there is room for improvement.

Concept Successes

  • check

  • check
    The nearby venues page does a through job of promoting the multitude of activities and venues available, particular in terms of ranking nearest first.

Scope for improvement

  • check
    The logo font with acitivity silhouettes isnt scalable for different resolutions and sizes.
  • check
    The torn headers on main pages: vouchers, activity, venue - while work in relation to the distressed brand concept of Geronigo, they ultimately cover too much valuable screen real estate without including enough above fold copy.
  • check
    Core UX principles, material as an example need applying to the website. The main navigation needs moving to the header, with the bottom navbar only used for mobile use.
  • check
    The ability to navigate quickly through activities to find a suitable activity and venue needs improvement.
  • check
    The core css framework needs to transition to bootstrap or similiar to ease maintenance issues and create a more robust core design layout.
  • check
    The underlying javascript needs improvement for speed benefits
