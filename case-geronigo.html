Geronigo | Case Study

The worlds first international adventure network

For transport theres Uber, for food theres Deliveroo, for beds theres Airbnb, for company in that bed theres tinder! But what about for the things to do? the activities, the adventures and excitment? Introducing Geronigo, 'One World... Every Adventure'.

Imagine you want to jump out of a plane at 14,000 feet, maybe you want to learn to scuba dive, to shoot your mates to bits on a paintball site, play clay pigeon shooting or go karting, bungee jumping, take all those awesome adventures and any others you can possibly think of and you will find them at Geronigo, on an international scale.

A renewed design concept for Geronigo

Below is the prototyping work for a proposed renewal of the Geronigo website, produced using Sketch & Marvel.

Pros

I believe the new UI features, such as the consistent title bars and tab menus ordered higher in the hierachy, in addition to columnised splash titles with content above the page fold give the site a more industry standard feel.

Cons

The adventurous edge of the site has been lost in the pursuit of a standardised UI. To maintain the initial concept of the website, further work needs undertaking to integrate the original torn graphical styling.

Features from the first development

Here are a few examples of key features implemented in the first iteration of the Geronigo website

How to display 100 activities and 5000 locations on one page?

It's not an easy task. But I think the first iteration of this was a success. The breadth of activities demonstrated pictorially are clearly displayed in the left column, closely stacked on top of one another. with the volume of related locations neatly nested in the aligned right column in a horizontal scrollable overflow list.

link Site Demonstration

Breaking free from a traditional full calendar availability booker

A new method was need to be designed to convey availability per date & session, without the need for a large and space filling calendar. A horizontal scrollable date picker with listed sessions below was design to modernise this issue.

Vertical real-estate was reduced by 50% and the features width allows for better rendering on lower width screen sizes in comparision to 7 day column calendars.

Notes from the beginning...

The concept was to take what had already been built with existing activity networks, but give it a bigger more authoritative feel to stand on the international stage.

Standardised design, but with an adventurous edge

The brand design needed to emote adventure and excitement, while keeping the UI accessible. How did I achieve this?

  • check
    Logo on a slant with a grunge effect font
  • check
    Torn graphical surrounds of main imagery
  • check
    Emphasis on keywords adventure & explore

Showcase breadth of 100 activities at 5000 locations

The key feature of the site is that you can't find this level of adventure anywhere else. How did I demonstrate that?

  • check
    A montage of activities pulling from the wide range of categories; air, water, combat, extreme to name a few.
  • check
    Animation of montage, to demonstrate joining together of widely different activities.
  • check
    Emphasis and repetition of the phrase 'Every Adventure'.

Limiting choice, leading to bookings

With so much choice, methods needed to be put in place to demonstrate realistic options for the user. How did I manage this?

  • check
    Using GEO location and pictorially list the closest venues nearby
  • check
    Creating a compact tabular list of activities, with venue columns ordered by nearest first.
  • check
    Putting availability details at the core of venue pages.
