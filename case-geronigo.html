Case Study

The worlds first international adventure network

For transport theres Uber, for food theres Deliveroo, for beds theres Airbnb, for company in that bed theres tinder! But what about for the things to do? the activities, the adventures and excitment? Introducing Geronigo, 'One World... Every Adventure'.

Imagine you want to jump out of a plane at 14,000 feet, maybe you want to learn to scuba dive, to shoot your mates to bits on a paintball site, play clay pigeon shooting or go karting, bungee jumping, take all those awesome adventures and any others you can possibly think of and you will find them at Geronigo, on an international scale.

How to display 100 activities and 5000 locations on one page?

It's not an easy task. But I think the first iteration of this was an initial success. The breadth of activities demonstrated pictorially are clearly displayed in the left column, closely stacked on top of one another. with the volumn of related locations neatly nested in the aligned right column in a horizontal scrollable overflow list.

Breaking free from a traditional full calendar availability booker

An appraisal of the current Geronigo

With the first iteration of Geronigo a lot has proven to work, but with any project there is room for improvement.

Concept Successes

  1. Logo font and angle represents the 'distressed' concept of the brand well
  2. Main montage acts as a good introduction with the right level of distressed design elements to convey adventure.
  3. Link structure and SEO are well defined and geared towards future growth.
  4. The nearby venues page does a through job of promoting the multitude of activities and venues available, particular in terms of ranking nearest first.
  5. The venue pages convey all require information in a well ordered pattern with booking the central feature.

Concept Failures

  1. The logo font with acitivity silhouettes isnt scalable for different resolutions and sizes.
  2. The torn headers on main pages: vouchers / activity / venue - while work in relation to the distressed brand concept of Geronigo, they ultimately cover too much valuable screen real estate.
  3. The core css framework needs to transition to bootstrap or similiar to ease maintenance issues and create a more robust core design layout.
  4. Core UX principles, material as an example need applying to the website. The main navigation needs moving to the header, with the bottom navbar only used for mobile use.
  5. The ability to navigate quickly through activities to find a suitable activity and venue needs improvement.
