Interview Talk Sheet

Questions for Interviewer -

Can you give me an insight into the design work you do?

What design applications do you use?

What skills are you looking for in your next designer?

What stages of prototyping are most important - lofi, medium, hifi or animation?

-- Is the office multilingual, can my Japanese level be overcome?

Potential questions for me -

What do you bring to the table as a designer?

  • I'm adaptable. I can design to a new environment by reviewing quickly the work of peers, analysising competitor design and forming related influences.
  • I'm resourceful. I can learn new skills quickly.
  • Experience as a web developer. I understand there may be boundaries.

What inspires you as a designer?

  • Creating something new that looks great, that wows me after repeated looks.
  • Exceeding somebodies expectations.
  • Developing non linear ideas.
  • Learning & Growing - tools, ideas, influences
  • Understanding the brief & knowing the user.

Tell us about your work history/experience?

  • I've worked for branches of the same company for 14 years
  • My skills are broad. I've worn many hats - UI design, web development & graphic design
  • In the last three years my role has been more focussed on the prototyping of new concepts for development - rather than splitting my work between design and development.

What UI skills do you have?

  • I'm a versatile designer - I can create good varied design, relaying on a wide range of influences to get me there.
  • In my recent work, I've worked with Sketch, Principle, Marvel, Figma. I'm exploring InVision Studio. I can learn these new applications very quickly.
  • I have a long history with Illustrator and Photoshop.
  • I have a good knowledge of materials guidelines and am exploring there website further.

Why do you want to leave your company?

  • I've felt a need to experience office life again
  • I want to pursue a career within Japan.
  • Gradually the appeal of remote working has diminished.
  • ----- The company I work for is experiencing difficulties due to the lockdown situation in England.

Can you adapt to a Japanese office environment?

  • Yes, I'm resourceful. It's important to quietly access and adapt to your environment and meet its needs.
  • I find Japanese etiquette relatable, my character sits between that of Japanese and British norms.
  • ----- In the last year I've made greater efforts to study Japanese.
